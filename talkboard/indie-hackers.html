How Indie Hackers Use TalkBoard: A Daily Workflow

TalkBoard lives in your Mac's menu bar. Three panels, three functions — all accessible via hotkeys. Here's what a typical indie hacker session looks like:

1

Dictate your launch post

You just shipped a feature. Instead of staring at a blank tweet composer, press ⌥⇧D and talk through what you built. TalkBoard transcribes on-device with whisper.cpp — no internet, no latency. The words appear wherever your cursor is. In 30 seconds, you've got a draft that captures how you actually talk about your product.

2

Polish with Quick Fix

The draft is raw — that's the point. Select the text, hit ⌥⇧F, and pick a tone: Formal for your changelog, Friendly for Twitter, Bullets for a Notion update. TalkBoard's on-device AI polishes it without changing your voice. No API key, no token costs, no cloud processing.

3

Clipboard as second brain

Throughout the day, every code snippet, email template, and marketing line you copy gets saved. Press ⌘⇧C and search your entire clipboard history. Pin your boilerplate — Stripe webhook snippets, shipping announcements, onboarding replies. AES-256 encrypted. Never leaves your Mac.

FAQ for Indie Hackers

Does TalkBoard replace Raycast Pro entirely?

It replaces the three features indie hackers use most from Raycast Pro: dictation, AI text polish, and clipboard history. If you also use Raycast's AI chat, cloud sync, or unlimited custom themes, you might want both. But if you were paying $10/month mostly for dictation + polish + clipboard — TalkBoard replaces all three for a one-time $9.99.

Is my data actually private?

Yes. Dictation runs whisper.cpp on-device. Text polish uses on-device AI (oMLX or Groq depending on your setup, both configurable). Clipboard history is AES-256 encrypted with a key stored in your macOS Keychain. TalkBoard never phones home — it works fully offline, and there's no telemetry, no analytics, no account system to even send data through.

Can I use this while screen recording or streaming?

Yes — and it's one of the quiet use-cases that indie hackers love. Dictation works without a visible floating UI (just a tiny menu bar indicator), and Quick Fix operates on selected text silently. You can dictate alt text, fix a typo in your demo script, or pull a link from clipboard history without breaking your recording flow.

What if I need to export my clipboard history?

The clipboard database is a local SQLite file with AES-256 encryption. While there's no one-click export (yet — it's on the roadmap), the storage is standard and accessible. The developer is an indie hacker shipping on a regular cadence; feature requests go directly to someone who actually ships.

How does this compare to Superwhisper?

Superwhisper is a great dictation-only tool at $8.50/month. TalkBoard is a three-in-one tool at $9.99 forever — dictation, AI polish, and clipboard manager in one menu bar icon. If all you need is dictation, Superwhisper is a solid pick. If you also need text polish and clipboard search, TalkBoard covers more ground for less money.
